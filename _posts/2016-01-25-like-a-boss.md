![Do these glasses make me look like a boss?](/images/2016/2016-01-25-like-a-boss.jpg)

Late last year I got new reading glasses. The first time my (then) three year old daughter saw me wearing them, she proclaimed "Daddy, you look like a boss!" After I got over my amusement, I asked her follow up questions to find what she really meant, but unfortunately wasn't able to get any greater detail.

That evening, her comment began to weigh on me. What does a *boss* look like? Why should a *boss* look like anyone in particular? What is a *boss*, really? **Am I prejudicing my daughter in favor of top down, centralized leadership styles that will forever stunt her growth in the freelancing digital nomad economy of the 21st century?**

The final question is a bit of a farce, but the sentiment is valid: some words simply imply an organizational style that may not be dominant or even exist in the future. Using the word *boss* implies someone is telling you what to do, and evaluating your output. The word *manager* is more flexible by implying someone optimizes your performance, but is still not ideal because people are not machines.

So, what should we call someone who is self-directed and collaborative; working alone or in teams; focused and creative; a leader and follower? The perfect holocracy participant? An oxymoron? (pun!)

**I call them a person.** Let's forget the management and self improvement books for a moment; it is all outdated. People are infinitely unique: strengths, weaknesses, hopes, and dreams all wrapped up in one package. The key to success in the future economy will be for people to connect with other people on a personal level to inspire them.
