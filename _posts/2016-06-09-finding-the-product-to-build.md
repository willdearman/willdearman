In my last post I explained why I need to build a product, but didn't answer what product to build. In fact, I was stuck for over a week on the issue of what to build. Initially it seemed like a green field, but I made progress once I realized there are quite a few constraints:
- Has to be something I have the skills and resources to build (sorry no gene sequencing or Hyperloop)
- Needs to be something with a short iteration cycle. While I could go the [route of a physical product](http://www.seanogle.com/guest-posts/amazon-fba), intuitively it feels like it would take too long to demonstrate that I am learning from feedback
- For me to stand out, my product needs to be something unique (no ride sharing, monthly box of menagerie, or fitness tracker for number of times you stand on your head)
- Extra points if the problem to be solved is interesting to me (sorry [dog poop monitoring mobile app](http://res.cloudinary.com/general-assembly-profiles/image/upload/v1460939434/lkjtewfe2etkg6nctysq.pdf))
- More points if is not just a product but a ["Whole New Way of Thinking"](https://hbr.org/2014/07/dont-sell-a-product-sell-a-whole-new-way-of-thinking/)


Ultimately I found my idea for a digital product, requiring minimal coding skills, doing something interesting, that brings into question the way most people do analysis...
