*This is a continuation from [Like a Boss? Part 1](/leadership/2016/01/25/like-a-boss/)*

Economies in the 19th and 20th century were defined by those who could commoditize things and people. From Henry Ford's famous quote "Any customer can have a car painted any color that he wants so long as it is black" to the application of Six Sigma to virtually every aspect of business.

Looking to the future, conformity will matter much less. If I am going to 3D print a case for my mobile phone and I want a power connector hole that is 2 mm larger, I can do that. Similarly, when I need the help of others to accomplish a goal, I will try to attract problem solvers not those who simply take instruction well.

I believe this more interactive method of creation will ultimately lead to solutions we cannot presently imagine. Why? The "Selectionist hypotheses" of [molecular evolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Molecular_evolution). **Simply, greater variation in composition (even mistakes) will lead to a stronger dominant outcome.** Referring back to my 3D printing example, if my larger power connector hole is in fact significantly better than the dominant design, more people will use it and ultimately my design will become dominant.

While this model is in its infancy, you can see it now by looking to the [fast fashion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fast_fashion) phenomena. Stylists and bloggers, rather than media behemoths, drive the definition of fashionable now, and they collaborate at a dramatically faster pace. This is not crowdsourcing where the preferences of the masses allow retailers to pick the most popular looks. This is constant evolution, which pushed consumer preferences to adapt faster, and thus had a quantifiable impact on clothing retailers (now stuck with unfashionable inventory).

I don't posit that fast fashion is better for humankind, but imagine that same phenomena being applied to the myriad of problems we face. I do posit that one hundred people will have more than one way to solve a problem, and the the existence of multiple solutions is exponentially better than having only one top down solution. 

Doing this will require a different model of management. So are you hoping your glasses make you look "like a boss"?
